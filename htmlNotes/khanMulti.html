Curl

Counterclockwise rotation is positive curl, Clockwise rotation is negative curl. Right hand rule. Up is positive, down is negative.

A curl can be generally defined as : curl(F) = ∇ × F

Curl takes in a vector field and outputs another vector field.

A divergence can then be defined as : div(F) = ∇ ⋅ F

Which takes a vector field but then outputs a scalar field

A gradient can be defined as : grad(f) = ∇f

A gradient takes a scalar and then gives a vector field

The formula for curl in two dimensions : $$\text{curl}(f (x,y)) = \frac{\partial f_y}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial f_x}{\partial y}$$

The formula for curl in three dimensions : $$\text{curl}(f (x,y,z)) = \hat{i} \left( \frac{\partial f_z}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial f_y}{\partial z} \right) - \hat{j} \left( \frac{\partial f_x}{\partial z} - \frac{\partial f_z}{\partial x}\right) + \hat{k} \left( \frac{\partial f_y}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial f_x}{\partial y} \right)$$

$$\text{curl} \, \mathbf{f} = \left( \frac{\partial f_z}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial f_y}{\partial z}, \frac{\partial f_x}{\partial z} - \frac{\partial f_z}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial f_y}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial f_x}{\partial y} \right )$$ Where P is the x−component of f and Q is the y−component.

The Laplacian of a scalar field f is the sum of each of its second partial derivatives :

$$\Delta f = \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2} + \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial z^2}$$ Δf = ∇2f = div∇f = div(grad(f)) Fields are harmonic if the Laplacian is zero

Jacobian Matrix and Determinant

$$\text{Suppose } f(x,y,z) = \begin{bmatrix} f_0 \\f_1\\f_2 \end{bmatrix} \text{ then the Jacobian is }$$ $$f(J) = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial x} \quad \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial y} \quad \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial z} \\ \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial x} \quad \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial y} \quad \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial z} \\ \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial x} \quad \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial y} \quad \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial z} \end{bmatrix}$$

Basically you just take a gradient across the rows. $$f(J) = \begin{bmatrix} \nabla f_0 \\ \nabla f_1 \\ \nabla f_2 \end{bmatrix}$$

This is super tedious to write work out for, just know that you can go across the matrix, doing one column at a time taking your derivatives of the three functions x, then the next column taking the derivatives of y, then the next taking it for z.

You’re also able to take the determinant of this function, which is the exact thing being ripped from those linear algebra classes with Senia.

As a reminder : $$\det(f) = \begin{bmatrix} a \quad b \\ c \quad d \end{bmatrix} = ad-bc$$. So specifically for this case, with a 2x2 matrix : $$\det J(f) = \det \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial x} \quad \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial y} \\ \\ \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x} \quad \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial y} \end{bmatrix} = \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial x}\frac{\partial f_1}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x} \frac{\partial f_0}{\partial y}$$

2nd Derivative testing to classify critical points

You can derive the qualities of a critical point quite easily using this simple derivation : H = fxxfyy − fxyfyx

  • H < 0 implies a saddle point

  • H > 0 and fxx > 0 implies a local minimum

  • H > 0 and fxx < 0 implies a local maximum

  • H = 0 means the test is inconclusive.

The scalar field f(x,y) = 2x2 − y2 + 4x + 2y − 1 has a critical point at (−1,1).

How does the second partial derivative test classify this critical point?

We will need to take a few double derivatives : $$\begin{aligned} f_{xx}&= \frac{\partial f}{\partial x \partial x} 2x^2-y^2+4x+2y-1 \\ &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} 4x+4 \\ &= 4 \\ f_{xy} &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} 2x^2-y^2+4x+2y-1 \\ &= 0\\ f_{yx}&= f_{xy} = 0\\ f_{yy}&= \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} 2x^2-y^2+4x+2y -1 \\ &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} -2y+2 \\ &= -2 \end{aligned}$$

Putting this all together : H = fxxfyy − fxyfyx H = (4)(−2) − (0)(0) H =  − 8

So we can conclude that this is a saddle point as H is negative.
