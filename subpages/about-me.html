Hello! I'm Dylan Sheehan, a 2nd year student attending Bates College in Lewiston Maine,

I'm currently a triple major in Physics, Chemistry, and Engineering at Bates on track to graduate in three years. This abnormal academic track has been led by a history of insatiable love for learning and experimentation. I've never been driven by grades, money, or anything material, rather I believe it the ultimate disservice to ones self to pass up the opportunity to learn as much as possible.

Throughout my life I always struggled with terrible handwriting due to a nerve impingement in my dominant arm. As such, during my freshman year of college I decided that if I was going to start taking notes during my courses I would need an alternative method of working.

So after doing some searching for a solution to my predicament I found some very interesting blogs and youtube videos, namely Gilles Castel's Blog (Rest in Peace ✝) and some helpful youtube videos introducing me to \(LaTeX\).This proved to be a massive turning point for me. By using Ultisnips and Vim I could quickly take course notes in beautiful formats at speeds comparable to hand writing.

However, I chose to wait for full adaptation of this set up as I was not sure if it would be sustainable in a college setting. So I instead chose to use Obsidian, a proprietary markdown viewer with MathJax support. After a full year of exclusively using this method, I've made the full switch to Vim and \(LaTeX\). I look to create a guide or two on how I did this, as my ultimate work flow is slightly different than those mentioned earlier. But those will come later on.
